About

My photography explores the textures of weathered and decaying wood - driftwood, fallen logs, moss-covered stumps. I'm drawn to how natural decay creates visual complexity: the grain lines, cracks, and patterns create abstract compositions where the eye can trace pathways and discover emerging forms.

Light and texture are my primary tools. My goal is to transform overlooked natural decay into something visually compelling that invites prolonged looking.

I work across environments - beaches, forests, wherever wood has been left to weather and break down. The material itself is the constant; the specific location matters less than what time and the elements have done to the surface.
